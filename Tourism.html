Rome is home to multiple tourist destinations that garners a lot of attention from travelers. Some of these destinations include, The Pantheon, The Colosseum and The Sistine Chapel.


The Pantheon

Originally built in 27 BC by statesmen Marcus Vipsanius Agrippa, rebuilt in 118 and 128 AD by emperor Hadrian. Until recent time was the largest dome built (142 feet); In 609 AD was dedicated the Chirch of Santa Maria Rotonda.

The Colosseum

This giant amphitheatre that was built under the Favian emperor. The amphitheatre seated 50 000 spectators. In the medieval times, the colosseum was used by two roman families, The Frangipane and the Annibaldi. The incredible sites still make a lasting impact with millions of visitors every year.

The Sistine Chapel

The Sistine Chapel is the pope’s official residence in Vatican City, in the Apolitical Palace. It was named by Pope Sixtus IV who had the chapel built from 1473 - 1481. The chapel’s ceiling is considered to be the one of the biggest pieces in high renaissance art. It was painted by Michelangelo using the fresco technique. It took him four years between 1508 and 1512.
